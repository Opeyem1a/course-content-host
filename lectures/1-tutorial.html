Application Scenario

Suppose we wish to develop a software application that allows users to track and categorize their spending habits. Since it may not be connected to any particular bank, let's say users are able to link their debit or credit cards to this web portal. Then all the transactions will appear on this website in some way. This software is meant to be useful for a wide variety of users. For example, high school students who have part time jobs and allowances may be one user group, and full time working adults between the age of 30-45 may be another user group.

Task 1: Identify a third user group for this application. Once you have it, create a persona for your user group and a persona for one of the user groups mentioned above.

Task 2: Suppose you named your two personas Jack and Jill. Separately, enumerate at least two user goals that Jack and Jill may have in using this software, at least two of each user skills and preferences that Jack and Jill may have that would influence how the software should be designed..

Task 3: After that, build a user scenario for each of the two personas. Your user scenarios should illustrate how differently the two personas are motivated to use the software application. Since the software has not been built, you may assume the software can have any meaningful features needed to achieve the necessary user goals.
